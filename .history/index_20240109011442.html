Our Goal

Save the Animals of the Planet

In Sri Lanka, the conservation of the diverse and unique wildlife is a paramount concern as the nation boasts a rich tapestry of flora and fauna. Efforts to save the animals of the planet in Sri Lanka focus on preserving the natural habitats and ecosystems that house a multitude of species, many of which are endemic to the island. Conservation initiatives collaborate with local communities to promote sustainable practices and raise awareness about the importance of protecting these invaluable creatures. From the majestic Asian elephant to the elusive leopard and the vibrant array of bird species, safeguarding the biodiversity of Sri Lanka is crucial for the well-being of the planet and the continuation of the country's ecological legacy. Through a combination of conservation policies, community engagement, and education, Sri Lanka is striving to ensure a harmonious coexistence between humans and the remarkable animals that call the island home.

  • Protected Areas

    Sri Lanka has established numerous national parks and wildlife sanctuaries to serve as protected areas for its diverse wildlife. These include Yala National Park, Wilpattu National Park, and Sinharaja Forest Reserve, among others.

  • Human-Wildlife Conflict Mitigation

    Sri Lanka faces challenges related to human-wildlife conflict, especially between elephants and local communities. Conservation efforts include the development of strategies to mitigate conflicts, such as the implementation of elephant corridors and the use of innovative methods to deter elephants from entering populated areas.

  • Endangered Species Conservation

    Conservation programs in Sri Lanka specifically target endangered species such as the Sri Lankan elephant, sloth bear, and several species of turtles. These initiatives involve monitoring populations, implementing anti-poaching measures, and promoting breeding programs where necessary.
